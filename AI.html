Deep Learning: Deep learning is a subset of machine learning that focuses on artificial neural networks with multiple layers. It enables the model to automatically learn hierarchical representations of data, leading to powerful pattern recognition capabilities.

  • learn the boundary between classes in the dataset.
  • learn a mapping from input to output (not the underlying probability distribution of the data)

  • learn the actual distribution of each class in the dataset.
  • generate new instances of data by using the underlying data distribution. (text, images, video, …)

Supervised Learning

Supervised Learning: This technique involves training AI models using labeled data, where the model learns patterns and relationships to make predictions or classify new, unseen data.

  • For each observation, we know the correct answer y = X




In 1880 Sir Francis Galton collected data on the length of sweet peas.




Together with Karl Pearson a mathematical model (algorithm) was created to predict the length of their offspring by the length of the parent.

Galton Conclusion: Prediction requires (quality) Data and Algorithm

  • simple linear Model 1 parameter
  • supervised learning (in & output are known, training & test set)
  • low data quality (outliers) may ruin the model: so cleaning or more data

Statistical modeling

Statistical modeling forms the foundation of many AI techniques and algorithms. It involves using mathematical and statistical methods to analyze data, identify patterns, and make predictions.

AI techniques

AI techniques, such as machine learning and deep learning, utilize statistical modeling approaches to learn from data, extract meaningful insights, and make predictions or decisions. In practice, statistical modeling and AI are often integrated.
